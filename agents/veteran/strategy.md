# Veteran Strategy

## As any role
- Round 1: ask questions, don't make accusations. Gather information first.
- Track who accused who first — the first accusation in a game is often a wolf testing the waters.
- Count votes carefully. Two players always voting together is a wolf tell.
- Never say "I'm a villager and I have nothing to hide" — it's a meaningless statement that wastes your speaking turn.

## As Werewolf
- Round 1: Ask pointed questions to seem analytical. Never defend yourself proactively — that's suspicious.
- Accuse someone who is already getting suspicion. Pile on, don't initiate.
- If your teammate is accused, don't defend them directly. Instead, redirect to a third player: "I think we're looking at the wrong person."
- Night kills: target the player who asked the best questions during the day. They are dangerous regardless of role.

## As Seer
- Night 1: inspect whoever speaks second. First speakers are performing; second speakers are reacting, which reveals more.
- Do NOT reveal on Day 1 unless you found a wolf. Revealing as "confirmed villager" wastes your cover.
- When you reveal: state your findings as facts, not claims. "I inspected X, they are a wolf" not "I think I might be the seer."
- If you found a wolf, wait until Round 2 of discussion to reveal — let them speak first so others can compare their Day 1 statements against your reveal.

## As Doctor
- Protect whoever spoke most analytically on Day 1 — they are likely the seer or a strong villager.
- Never protect the same player twice. Wolves will test this.
- If someone claims seer, do NOT protect them immediately — wolves might be baiting you into a predictable pattern. Protect them the night AFTER.
- Never reveal as doctor unless you are about to be voted out and need to prove your value.

## As Villager
- Your job is to generate information through questions, not accusations.
- Ask specific questions: "Agent X, who do you think we should kill tonight and why?"
- If someone deflects your question, note it publicly. "I notice Agent X didn't answer my question."
- On vote: never vote for someone just because they're quiet. Vote for someone whose statements don't add up.
